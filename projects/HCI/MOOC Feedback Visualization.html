Abstract

Importance of Feedback.

Learner feedback on the ongoing course content is a valuable resource for the Course instructors in the absence of in-person interaction. With the increasing popularity of Massive open online courses (MOOCs), the usage of Video Content in the Online learning environment serves as a major resource to the course instructors as well as course developers.

Why Video content?

Video content in a learning environment caters for multiple roles such as a video lecture or student coursework medium. Due to the increase in the number of course enrollments, educators of MOOCs are trying to keep up by developing a variety of courses in several domains. Due to the rapid development of the video content as a learning source for these courses, educators often find it to be progressively hard to pick up a more profound understanding of the correctness and relevance of video content, as well as the learner feedback about it.

Our Direction.

In order for the instructor to get precise feedback about the course video content and at the same time identify valuable suggestions or detect errors, we created a learning analytics system that maps the individual learner feedback to the course video frames location, thereby allowing to visualize the feedback points using heatmap.

This system would allow instructors to improve the quality of MOOC course content while it's deployed live on the Online learning platform, therefore ensuring continuous improvement of the video content through the usage of a novel feedback methodology.

Solution

Our Approach.

Focus question - How can we make our current learner feedback methodologymore efficient and precise?

Ideology.

For devising a Feedback system for the MOOC platforms (See System Flow), we accepted the ideology proposed by Daradoumis et. al [9]. They proposed a framework which utilizes software agents with the objective to improve management, delivery and evaluation of MOOCs. Analogous to their system flow pipeline, our approach also follows a three phase procedure for complete function and information flow that allows the learners to provide feedback which the instructors or course developers can use for either iterative improvement of course or immediate action.

Assessment
and Learning.

Assessment and Learning is the set of tasks that the learner community of the system is supposed to be carrying out. It basically represents the perspective of the Learner during the learning activity. A typical learner engages in the learning activity by firstly getting the access to the MOOC platform via successful login and then moving onto the course portal and selecting the course in which he/she is enrolled. The video lectures are typically delivered through a generic video delivery interface (See Figure 2) after the topic selection.

The learner can then play the video lecture as through common video playback controls. During this video lecture playback if the learner faces any sort of problem or confusion in the MOOC video content or if they want to report some sort of an error and therefore if they wish to express it in the form of a feedback, they can click on the doubtful part of the video frame area and the coordinates will be recorded along with the time-instance of the frame in milliseconds. Further, on the right side column of the screen, a feedback form would be generated. The feedback form would contain a few input fields. Through the feedback form, the learner can fill out a feedback message and specify the type of feedback through a dropdown input. On the submit of feedback form, all the information like Feedback origin location (Frame Coordinates + Frame Time Instance) is tagged together and submitted as a record in the database.

Simply said, each user click for the feedback corresponds to a five valued (x, y, time, feedbacktype, feedbackmessage) point in the video lecture. All these feedback points data from a unique learner can then be combined with the feedback data of the other learners about the same video and stored together for the analysis phase. With this aggregation of learner feedback data points about the video lecture, a heatmap visualization overlay is created in order to better identify the prioritized feedback action points.


Delivery.

Delivery part refers to the process of setting up the environment for the learner to engage in the learning. This part is carried out by the MOOC Platform. The entire responsibility of displaying the video content to the logged-in learner depending upon the request being made is handled by the MOOC Platform interface. The interface varies from one platform to the other but the general video display environment remains more or less the same and only varies in the video content depending upon the selected course. Apart from this, the aggregation of the feedback click points collected from the course learners is also carried out in this phase. Using this collection of data, the MOOC Platform generates and offers an analytics dashboard to the instructor for looking at the feedback in an intuitive manner. Further, this raw dataset can be downloaded by the instructor for external analysis.

Analysis and Design.

Analysis and Design part consists of the set of responsibilities and the umbrella of tasks that the Course Instructor or mentor is supposed to be carrying out. This basically represents the perspective of the instructor while working on the system. The instructor of the course developer firstly designs a new course following the curriculum. Once the course content is completely designed and the course is deployed on the MOOC Platform, learners are allowed to enroll in the course and assess the video content. The feedback collected would then be shown on the instructor interface as an analytics dashboard with all the statistics. This is where the analytics phase comes into the picture. The instructor can then determine the feedback they need to focus on through this visualization dashboard and modify or improve the video content based on that. Reflections

Final Thoughts.

We proposed a MOOC Feedback visualization system based on a three stage pipeline consisting of Design & Analysis, Delivery and Assessment. It implements an iterative flow between these three stages. In the present MOOC environment, the growing number of MOOC participants/learners and corresponding increasing number of courses, make it considerably difficult to maintain the quality of MOOC Video content. In such a situation, course instructors or mentors are typically dependent on the feedback from learners for improvement of the course content and also to fix the errors (if any) in the content. Current methods for feedback are extremely trivial in nature and are essentially text-message based and lack three most important features of any feedback system which are Precision/Specificity, Prioritization and Detection of genuine feedback.

Summary.

Our feedback system which utilizes heatmap visualization for feedback points representation tags the feedback origin location within the video to the actual feedback message while submitting the feedback. Later these feedback points are aggregated together to give instructor a prioritized overview of the confusion/problem points. Since these feedback points are crowdsourced from the learner community, the detection of genuine and non-genuine feedback becomes simpler and practical by determining regions of high density and low density on the heatmap overlay.
